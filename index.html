Academic profile

Language technology with global reach and local purpose.

Arbi Haza Nasution is a computational linguistics and natural language processing scholar whose current research focuses on LLMs and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for multilingual and low-resource language understanding. He earned his Ph.D. in Social Informatics from Kyoto University (2018) and his M.IT. in Management Information Systems (2012) and B.IT. in Computer Science (2010) from the National University of Malaysia (Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia, UKM). He is currently a Professor in the Department of Informatics Engineering, Universitas Islam Riau, Indonesia. His research centers on bilingual lexicon induction, multilingual machine translation, and language technologies for low‑resource languages. In recognition of his contributions to inclusive language technologies and the preservation of low-resource languages, he was invited by UNESCO to participate in the first Language Technologies for All (LT4All) conference in 2021, and later again in 2025 for the second LT4All at UNESCO Headquarters in Paris.

Publications

Selected publications, organized by research contribution.

The research develops along a connected trajectory: from building bilingual lexical resources and machine-translation methods for multilingual and low-resource languages, to advancing classification and applied AI across social, environmental, financial, and industrial domains. This foundation now supports research on LLM evaluation, AI security and adversarial robustness, and retrieval-augmented knowledge systems—contributing toward language technologies that are more reliable, explainable, inclusive, and relevant to real-world needs.
